Pitt Street

One-bedroom apartment in Honeyman and Keppie warehouse

  • Use: Airbnb Superhost property

Investment Success

  • Maintained Superhost status throughout tenure
  • Provided income for career retraining
  • £38,350 capital appreciation
  • Historic warehouse conversion
  • Prime city location

Project Story

A one-bedroom apartment in a warehouse designed by Honeyman and Keppie with beautiful arched windows. This project was strategically purchased to create an income-generating Airbnb property while providing capital appreciation. The renovation focused on creating a welcoming space for short-term guests while honoring the building's industrial heritage.

Renovation Programme

Interior Improvements

  • Complete redecoration throughout the apartment
  • Kitchen replacement and modernization
  • Breakfast bar area created by the window
  • Focus on maximizing natural light from arched windows

Airbnb Optimization

  • Design focused on guest comfort and functionality
  • Strategic layout to maximize space efficiency
  • Quality finishes to ensure positive reviews
  • Maintenance-friendly materials and surfaces

Architectural Heritage

Honeyman and Keppie were one of Scotland's most important architectural partnerships, with Charles Rennie Mackintosh as a partner. The warehouse conversion preserved the beautiful arched windows that characterize the building's industrial heritage while adapting the space for modern residential use.

Investment Strategy

This apartment was bought to rent out on Airbnb and provided an income for both James and I to re-train in our chosen fields throughout the time we rented it out. We maintained Superhost status for our whole tenure as hosts, demonstrating the success of our design approach in creating spaces that guests loved.

The property appreciation from £114,000 to £152,350 over two years, combined with the rental income, made this an excellent example of how thoughtful renovation can create both immediate income and long-term capital growth.

Guest Experience Design

The breakfast bar by the arched windows became a favorite feature for guests, providing a perfect spot to enjoy morning coffee while taking in views of the city. The design balanced the industrial character of the warehouse conversion with the comfort and amenities that short-term rental guests expect.
