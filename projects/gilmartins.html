Gilmartins

Victorian Building - Former Bank of Scotland Branch Conversion

Gilmartins Cafe - Hero Image Placeholder

Project Details

  • Property Type: Commercial conversion
  • Original Use: Bank of Scotland branch
  • New Use: Tea room & cafe
  • Building Era: Victorian
  • Renovation Cost: Approx £40,000
  • Status: Business premises (rented)

Special Features

  • Ca Pietra scalloped tiles coffee bar
  • Traditional gilded window signage
  • Commercial kitchen conversion
  • Upholstered bench seating
  • Two-unit expansion
  • Victorian character preservation

Project Story

A former Bank of Scotland branch set in a Victorian building presented a unique opportunity to create a charming cafe that honored the building's heritage while serving the local community. The conversion required extensive planning and specialist installations to transform a financial institution into a welcoming tea room.

Conversion Programme

Infrastructure & Services

  • Three-phase power installation
  • Sound-proof plasterboard and insulation
  • Full new electrical board
  • Complete plastering throughout

Kitchen & Service Areas

  • Tea room reconfigured to create small commercial kitchen
  • Stainless steel worktops and bakery appliances fitted
  • Coffee bar commissioned and clad in Ca Pietra scalloped tiles
  • Till, coffee machine and knock box integration

Customer Areas & Facilities

  • Two existing toilets reconfigured to form one new WC
  • Intervening ventilated space with baby changing facilities
  • Window replaced where cash machine had previously been
  • Traditional gilded signage applied to windows

Expansion Phase

  • Late 2019 expansion into neighbouring unit
  • Rebuilding of stairs required
  • Removal of fireproof plasterboard
  • Upholstered bench seating for large dining room

Design Philosophy

The conversion of Gilmartins focused on preserving the Victorian character of the building while creating a functional, welcoming cafe space. The traditional gilded signage reflected the age and dignity of the building, while modern amenities ensured customer comfort and operational efficiency.

The custom coffee bar with Ca Pietra scalloped tiles became a focal point, combining contemporary design with traditional craftsmanship. The expansion into the neighboring unit demonstrated how heritage buildings can adapt and grow while maintaining their essential character.
