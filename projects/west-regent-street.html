Project Story

A top-floor one-bedroom apartment in De Quincey House, Glasgow, a conversion from an Alfred Waterhouse building originally built for the Prudential insurance company. This project showcased how thoughtful design can maximize the potential of architectural heritage while creating a comfortable modern living space.

Renovation Programme

Storage & Built-ins

  • Alcove shelving installed to make use of deep chimney breast alcoves
  • Custom storage solutions maximizing period features

Kitchen & Living Areas

  • New flooring laid throughout
  • Kitchen peninsula unit created
  • Dining nook designed and installed

Lighting Design

  • New lighting circuits installed
  • Pool of light created by the bed for ambiance
  • Dining area lighting for atmosphere

Bathroom Transformation

  • New bathroom installed
  • Deep Japanese soaking tub fitted
  • Crosswater brassware throughout

Design Philosophy

The renovation of this Alfred Waterhouse apartment focused on honoring the building's prestigious architectural heritage while creating a highly functional modern home. The deep chimney breast alcoves, characteristic of period buildings, were transformed from challenging spaces into beautiful storage features.

The Japanese soaking tub represented a fusion of Eastern wellness philosophy with Western architectural tradition, creating a luxurious retreat within the historic building. The kitchen peninsula design maximized the compact space while the dining nook provided intimate dining within the apartment's period proportions.

Heritage Context

Alfred Waterhouse, renowned for buildings like the Natural History Museum in London, designed the original Prudential building with characteristic attention to both grandeur and functionality. Our renovation respected this legacy while adapting the space for contemporary urban living.
