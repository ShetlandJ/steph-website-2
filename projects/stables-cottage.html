Stables Cottage

Stewarton - 18th-century former stables, Lainshaw House estate

Project Details

  • Property Type: Former stables conversion
  • Location: Lainshaw Woods, Stewarton
  • Era: 18th-century
  • Purchase Price: £190,000 (2020)
  • Renovation Cost: £40,000
  • Sale Price: £250,000 (2023)

Key Features

  • New herringbone flooring throughout
  • Freestanding bath in bedroom
  • Built-in wardrobes
  • Three-level landscaped garden
  • Heritage property restoration
  • Woodland setting

Project Story

Stables Cottage, a former stables attached to the estate of Lainshaw House, is an 18th-century property on the border of Lainshaw Woods in Stewarton. This unique heritage property required careful restoration to honor its historic character while creating a comfortable modern home.

Renovation Programme

Interior Transformations

  • New herringbone flooring throughout downstairs
  • New boiler installation
  • Full redecoration throughout
  • Built-in wardrobes installed in both bedrooms
  • Bathroom renovated and freestanding bath installed in bedroom

Garden & Exterior

  • Garden completely relandscaped to form three levels of tiled patios
  • Raised bed for planting created
  • Integration with woodland setting

Heritage Considerations

Working with an 18th-century former stables presented unique challenges and opportunities. The renovation respected the building's agricultural heritage while transforming it into a comfortable residential space. The woodland setting on the Lainshaw House estate provided a stunning natural backdrop that influenced our design choices.

The Result

The transformation of Stables Cottage resulted in a significant increase in value from £190,000 to £250,000, demonstrating how thoughtful renovation of heritage properties can honor their history while creating contemporary comfort.
